{# Our Problem #}

Our Problem

Unlike other open-source software, Dingo was started out as a personal project of its author, a non-designer non-artist programmer. Therefore, all the icons that are distributed with the currently latest release of Dingo (except for the main logo, which was designed by Custom Icon Design) are taken from either GNOME 3 or other free software packages. This is really not a good thing and so we need help from volunteer designers and artists.

{# Available Icons #}

Available Icons

The full list of icons is available through our Github repository. You can also download the latest development version of Dingo to see these icons. All of the icons are in need to be redesigned or replaced with something better-looking and unique to Dingo.

{# Design Guidelines #}

Design Guidelines

Please keep in mind that Dingo uses the Gtkmm (GTK+) library for its user-interface. This means that Dingo's icons should be designed in a way that fit with the GNOME icons theme. Please read Designing Effective Icons and art.gnome.org before getting started.


If you have some new ideas on how the icons in Dingo should look like, or even better, have completed some icons specially tailored for Dingo, please do not hesitate to contact us through our mailing-list (see the following section).

{# Submitting Icons #}

Submitting Icons

It is important to note that if you decide to design and submit icons for Dingo you must include the following in your email to our mailing-list, or your works will not be accepted:

  1. The scalable version of the icon (required)
  2. The non-scalable versions of the icon in the following dimension (optional):
    16 x 16, 24 x 24, 32 x 32, 48 x 48, 128 x 128, 256 x 256
  3. Your full name (required)
  4. Your email address (required)
  5. Your website URL (optional)

After approving these icons, we will attach all of your submitted information into the releases that use your icons and the Contributors List on this site.
