{# History #}

History

Dingo was originally developed and released in 2012 by Phong V. Cao, an undergraduate college student at Stony Brook University. The software was created with the solely purpose of helping Linux users manage and enjoy their audios and videos collections in a simple yet effective way.

{# Technical Summary #}

Technical Summary

Dingo is written in C/C++ using GNOME technologies (Gtkmm, GStreamermm, libxml++, libnotify, gettext). It takes advantage of other non-GNOME open-source libraries (Boost, MediaInfo, SQLite3) and applies various design patterns such as Observer, Delegation, Facade and Model-Delegate, a variation of the Model-View-Controller (MVC) pattern. Dingo is free/open-source software, released under the GNU General Public License v3.0.
